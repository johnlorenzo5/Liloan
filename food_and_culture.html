Food and Culture

Don’t miss the chance to try local flavors! Tasting traditional dishes is the best way to explore the culture, history, and heart of any place you visit in Liloan, Cebu.

Masi

Craving something chewy and sweet? Try Liloan’s masi! These delightful rice treats are filled with rich peanut paste and are easy to find around the town plaza or along the highway.

Rosquillos

First crafted by Margarita 'Titay' T. Frasco in 1907, this Liloan favorite boasts a crunchy shell, sweet aroma, and delightful taste. Enjoy it your way—dip it in a hot drink or twirl it around your fingers!

Patatas

Patatas: a nostalgic treat that brings back childhood memories. These crunchy, box-shaped snacks are a perfect alternative to salty chips—crispy, satisfying, and oh-so-familiar!

Otap

Otap: a sweet, oval-shaped puff pastry dusted with sugar. Its crumbly, delicate texture adds to the joy of every bite—a true treat for sweet lovers!.

Salvaro

Salvaro, a thin and crispy coconut snack, is so addictive it often sells out, says vendor Aling Tusing. A true Filipino delicacy you can’t resist!

Caycay

"Caycay: a crunchy layered biscuit coated in toasted peanuts. Inspired by Spanish influences, it has evolved into the beloved snack we enjoy today!
